You have questions, we have answers.
What’s a credit report?
Your credit report is a record of your financial history. It includes credit inquiries, open and closed credit accounts, your payment history and bankruptcies (if applicable). It also contains personal information such as your current and former address(es), names and employers. All this information is part of your data identity. By managing it regularly, you can help ensure you’re accurately represented in the modern economy so you can get the credit opportunities you desire.
How often is my credit score updated?
Your credit score is typically updated monthly based on the information provided by the credit bureaus. However, significant changes in your financial activity (such as late payments or new credit accounts) may cause more frequent updates.
What factors affect my credit score?
Several factors affect your credit score, including payment history, credit utilization, length of credit history, types of credit accounts, and recent credit inquiries. Maintaining healthy habits in these areas can improve your score over time.
Can I dispute errors on my credit report?
Yes, you can dispute errors on your credit report. Contact the credit bureau that issued the report and provide supporting documentation to correct any inaccuracies. The process typically takes 30–45 days.
Is my credit score the same across all bureaus?
No, your credit score may vary slightly between the three major credit bureaus (TransUnion, Experian, and Equifax) due to differences in their scoring models and the data they collect. However, the scores are generally close to each other.
